## 1) Brand Strategy

### 1.1 Mission

Eliminate noise and enable verified, privacy‑first autonomy in physical security by fusing multi‑modal sensing, AI at the edge, and human oversight.

### 1.2 Vision

A world where critical facilities operate with **trusted autonomy**: real threats are confirmed in seconds, actions are auditable, and privacy is preserved by default.

### 1.3 Core Principles

1. **Evidence over hype:** Claims map to measurable outcomes (false positive reduction, sub‑minute workflows, audit logs).
2. **Privacy by design:** Identities masked until verified; data processed on‑site; role‑based access and retention controls.
3. **Edge autonomy:** Operates locally during WAN outages; low latency by default.
4. **Human‑in‑the‑loop:** Automation proposes; humans approve interventions above defined risk thresholds.
5. **Open, not locked‑in:** Overlays existing cameras/VMS/ACS; stable APIs and adapters.

### 1.4 Brand Promise

**"Reliable alerts, not noise."** Sub‑minute, verified, and privacy‑respecting.

### 1.5 Reasons to Believe (public‑safe)

* Multi‑sensor fusion (video + thermal + radar, with optional RF/acoustic) to corroborate events.
* Edge processing for speed, resilience, and compliance.
* Evidence pipeline with hashing, redaction, and audit controls.
* Open integrations with major VMS/ACS and situational‑awareness networks.

## 4) Visual Identity System

### 4.1 Logo

* **Primary wordmark:** "Tessure". Clean geometric sans; custom "T" terminal.
* **Monogram:** "T" built from interlocking sensor‑arcs (fusion motif).
* **Clear space:** ≥ 1× cap‑height around logo.
* **Minimum size:** Wordmark ≥ 120 px width on screen; 24 mm in print.
* **Do not:** distort, add effects, rotate, recolor outside palette, place on low‑contrast backgrounds.

### 4.2 Color Palette (Light Theme - accessible, clean + professional)

**Base neutrals (Light Theme)**

* **Pure White** #FFFFFF (primary background)
* **Frost** #F8FAFC (slate-50, secondary background)

* **Graphite** #64748B (slate-500, secondary text)

**Semantic tokens**

* **Background Primary:** #FFFFFF
* **Background Secondary:** #F8FAFC
* **Background Tertiary:** #F1F5F9
* **Border Default:** #E2E8F0
* **Border Emphasis:** #CBD5E1

* **Text Secondary:** #64748B

* **Accent Hover:** #1E3A8A

**Notes:** All combinations maintain WCAG 2.2 AA contrast minimum; Trust Blue (#1E40AF) provides 7:1 contrast on white backgrounds; text colors ensure readability on light backgrounds.

### 4.3 Typography

* **Primary:** Inter (UI/system‑friendly). Weights: 400, 500, 600, 700.
* **Secondary (monospace):** IBM Plex Mono for code/diagnostics.
* **Fallbacks:** `Inter, ui-sans-serif, system-ui, -apple-system, Segoe UI, Roboto, Helvetica, Arial`
* **Web sizes:** H1 40/48, H2 28/36, H3 22/28, Body 16/24, Small 14/20.
* **Print sizes:** H1 36 pt, H2 24 pt, Body 10–11 pt.

### 4.4 Iconography & Illustration

* Line icons with 2 px stroke on web, 1 pt in print; rounded joins.
* Fusion motif: overlapping arcs/dots to suggest corroboration.
* Technical diagrams: isometric nodes with labeled sensor inputs; keep colors within palette.
* Icons use Charcoal (#334155) on light backgrounds for optimal visibility.

### 4.5 Photography & Video

* Environments: perimeter fences, gates, control rooms, rooftops; daylight and dusk shots, weather diversity.
* Style: candid operational shots; avoid staged security theater.
* People: authentic operators/technicians, diverse representation, no identifiable faces unless consented; blur or mask where appropriate.
* B‑roll: sensor close‑ups, edge node installs, dashboard interactions.
* Lighting: prefer natural daylight or well-lit environments to match light theme aesthetic.

### 4.6 Data Visualization

* Use Trust Blue (#1E40AF) for primary data, Success Green (#10B981) for secondary, Graphite (#64748B) for support.
* Light gridlines in Steel (#E2E8F0); clear axis labels in Charcoal (#334155).
* Avoid rainbow palettes; maintain AA contrast on all labels and data points.
* Background: Pure White or Frost for chart areas.

### 4.7 Layout & Grids

* **Web/App grid:** 12‑col, 24 px gutters; section spacing 64–96 px.
* **Print grid:** 6‑col, 4 mm gutters; margins 12–18 mm.
* **Card pattern:** Icon + title + 2–3 lines body + CTA; cards use Frost background with Steel borders.

### 4.8 Motion

* Ease: standard ease‑out; durations 120–280 ms.
* Page transitions subtle; avoid parallax on operational pages.
* Micro‑interactions for verify/approve affordances; respect reduced‑motion settings.

## 5) Verbal Identity & Editorial Style

### 5.1 Voice

* **Confident, clear, grounded.**
* **Technical but human.**
* **Privacy‑first and ethical.**

### 5.2 Tone by Context

* **Website & sales:** crisp, outcome‑led.
* **Docs & integration:** precise, schematic.
* **PR & policy:** responsible, transparent.
* **Social:** expert, helpful, never sensational.

### 5.3 Language Do/Don't

**Do:** verified response, evidence, edge autonomy, human‑in‑the‑loop, fusion, overlay, privacy by design.
**Don't:** kill chain, geofence (unless required), poly‑defense, militaristic tropes; avoid over‑claims ("zero false negatives").

### 5.4 Editorial Mechanics

* Use plain English; active voice.
* Numerals for stats; spell out one–nine if not technical.
* Oxford comma; US spelling; sentence case headlines.
* Inclusive language; avoid surveillance‑heavy phrasing.
