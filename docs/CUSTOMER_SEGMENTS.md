# Customer Segments

Five primary segments, ranked by ACV and wedge strength for Year 1. Each section lists **trigger events** (what makes them buy this quarter) and **language that resonates** (verbatim phrasing from public RFPs, analyst reports, and trade-press interviews).

---

## 1. Critical infrastructure operators

**Accounts:** investor-owned utilities (Duke, Southern, Dominion, AEP, PG&E), municipal utilities, T&D operators, water authorities, pipeline operators (Enbridge, Kinder Morgan, Williams), telecom central offices.

**ACV profile:** $250k–$2M per site-cluster; multi-year. Sites-per-customer: 10–3,000. A single mid-size utility is a **$10M+ ARR account** on full roll-out.

**Buyer titles:** VP Security, Director Physical Security, CSO, Chief Compliance Officer, VP T&D Operations.

**Triggers:**
- NERC CIP-014 revision cycle (every 3 years) or audit finding.
- TSA Pipeline Security Directive compliance gap.
- Recent substation attack in operator's ISO/RTO (Metcalf 2013, NC Moore County 2022, WA Tacoma 2023, FL Duke 2023).
- E-ISAC bulletin or DHS CISA advisory naming their sector.
- Insurance renewal with loss-control requirement to demonstrate "verified response" capability.
- Drone incident at any of their facilities.

**Language that resonates:**
- "NERC CIP-014 physical security requirements"
- "verified perimeter response" (vs "alarm monitoring")
- "evidence-grade incident logging"
- "no rip-and-replace of existing VMS"
- "CISA-aligned architecture"
- "TSA-compliant reporting"

**Don't use:**
- "kill chain," "hunter-killer," militarized framing
- "autonomous response" without heavy human-in-the-loop qualifiers

**Sales motion:** top-down. 6–12 month cycle. Pilot 3–5 sites, then roll out 25–100. Procurement through Carahsoft or direct.

## 2. Hyperscale & colocation data centers

**Accounts:** AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, Meta (hyperscale); Equinix, Digital Realty, CyrusOne, QTS, Stack (colo); NTT, Iron Mountain.

**ACV profile:** $150k–$500k per campus. Mid-size colo has 10–40 campuses; hyperscale 100+.

**Buyer titles:** Head of Physical Security, Global Security Operations Director, Site Security Lead, Director of Data Center Operations (dual-report).

**Triggers:**
- New site build or expansion (CapEx project, security spec written at design time).
- Insurance audit or finding.
- Tailgating incident.
- Cyber team pushes for physical+cyber correlation (recent in hyperscale: port-scan + physical intrusion pattern).
- Move from managed guard-force vendor (Allied Universal, Securitas) to in-house SOC.

**Language that resonates:**
- "uptime SLA protection"
- "verified tailgating detection"
- "cyber-physical correlation"
- "defense in depth"
- "24/7 GSOC tooling"
- "customer audit-ready reporting" (for colo, critical — their customers audit them)

**Sales motion:** mid-funnel. 3–9 months. Pilot at 1 site, expand. Often procurement-led through integrator (Convergint, ADT Commercial, Johnson Controls).

## 3. Logistics, freight yards, ports

**Accounts:** Prologis (landlord to 5000+ tenants), FedEx/UPS/DHL/Amazon hubs, intermodal operators (CSX, UP, BNSF), port operators (POLA/POLB terminals, NY/NJ).

**ACV profile:** $40k–$150k per yard. ORCA (organized retail crime + cargo theft) is the line item they're spending on.

**Buyer titles:** Director of Security, Loss Prevention Manager, VP Operations, Director of Transportation Security.

**Triggers:**
- Cargo theft incident ($100k+ loss) on their yard or a peer yard.
- Insider theft investigation.
- Insurance renewal citing loss frequency.
- Amazon/Walmart/big-box customer audit of their 3PL's security posture.
- Port Authority mandate post-incident.

**Language that resonates:**
- "after-hours perimeter verification"
- "cargo theft prevention" or "ORCA defense"
- "insider threat detection"
- "RFID + camera + fence fusion"
- "loss prevention ROI"
- "chain-of-custody evidence"

**Sales motion:** 2–4 month cycle. Easier pilot path. Often 1 yard first, then 5–20 across the portfolio.

## 4. Private-wealth estates & family offices

**Accounts:** UHNW residences (Aspen, Jackson Hole, Palm Beach, Montecito, Nantucket, Hamptons), corporate family offices, celebrity compounds.

**ACV profile:** $30k–$80k per estate. Tight-knit referral market.

**Buyer titles:** Estate Security Director, Family Office COO, Personal Security Detail Lead.

**Triggers:**
- Paparazzi or protester incident.
- Insurance carrier mandate (Chubb Masterpiece, AIG Private Client, PURE Group).
- Security consultant retainer engagement (TAL, Pinkerton, Gavin de Becker).
- Principal travel incident creating anxiety.
- Neighbor installs something visible.

**Language that resonates:**
- "privacy-first" (non-negotiable for this segment)
- "discreet perimeter coverage"
- "staff-friendly, not panopticon"
- "insurance-approved"
- "no footage leaves site"
- "masked identities until verified threat"

**Sales motion:** relationship-driven through security consultants and boutique integrators. 1–3 month cycle. Higher-touch install, lower ACV, but recurring + referrals.

---

## 5. Resorts, marinas, event venues

**Accounts:** luxury resort chains (Auberge, Rosewood, Aman), marinas (IGY, Safe Harbor), sports & festival venues (AEG, Live Nation, MSG).

**ACV profile:** $50k–$200k per property, or event-rental $20k–$80k per engagement.

**Buyer titles:** Director of Security, VP Operations, Event Security Lead.

**Triggers:**
- UAS incident (drone filming guests, swimming near yachts).
- Crowd incident at peer venue (Astroworld 2021 as precedent).
- VIP booking requiring demonstrated security posture.
- Insurance premium increase tied to incident history.

**Language that resonates:**
- "guest privacy preserved"
- "crowd-density management"
- "drone detection + track"
- "event overlay — deploy for a weekend, remove Monday"
- "insurance-rated"

**Sales motion:** often first sale is a rental/temporary install for a high-profile event. Converts to permanent if it solves a real problem.

---

## Segment prioritization for Year 1

**Start with #3 (logistics) and #2 (colo data centers).** Shorter cycles, clearer ROI numbers, more tolerant of a 6-person-team vendor. **#1 (critical infra) is the biggest ACV but 12-month cycles and federal-grade procurement.** Win 2–3 #3/#2 logos in Year 1; use them as proof for #1 in Year 2.

**#4 (estates) is a parallel lane** — lower ACV but referral-driven and can break even on one consultant relationship. Run as a side channel.

**#5 (resort/event) is opportunistic** — take rental work when offered, don't build a sales team around it.
