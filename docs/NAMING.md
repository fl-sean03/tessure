Working name **Tessure** is validated against the playbook Phase 2 criteria. This doc records the exercise, candidate tiers, domain and handle availability as of 2026-04-14, and what to reserve immediately.

## What should the name signal (and avoid)

**Signal:**
- Trusted enterprise infrastructure, not weapons-platform vibe.
- Coverage, corroboration, evidence (the fusion metaphor).
- Calm confidence; a word a risk officer or utility CSO could say to their board without it sounding like a defense vendor.
- Works as a noun ("Tessure"), as an adjective ("Tessure-verified"), and as a product prefix ("Tessure Fusion Node").

**Avoid:**
- Militarized roots (aegis, bulwark, sentinel, rampart, phalanx) — triggers the "defense-tech cosplay" trap.
- AI-novelty (anything ending in -ify, -io, -ly, or with "AI" in the root).
- Descriptive compounds (FuseGrid, SafeEdge, VerifyOne) — category-generic, forgettable, hard to trademark.
- Squatted/contested dictionary words (Verity, Attest, Arcus, Citadel) — all premium .coms taken, many with incumbent usage.

## Trademark scan (preliminary)

USPTO TSDR and Trademarkia were both rate-limiting this session (403 / 429). A proper trademark search requires:

1. **USPTO TESS** direct search on "TESSURE" + phonetic variants (TESSUR, TESSURA, TESSERA) in Classes 9 (software/hardware), 42 (SaaS), 45 (security services).
2. **International:** EU, UK, Canada (the first three markets beyond US).
3. **Common-law search:** Google + Bing + LinkedIn for any commercial use of "Tessure" in physical security, defense, or adjacent categories.

**Informal check (this session):**
- No widely visible commercial use of "Tessure" in physical-security surfaces online outside Sean's own archive page (seanflorez.com/archive/tessure).
- tessure.com registrant is anonymized via NameCheap WHOIS privacy; dormant.
- /company/tessure on LinkedIn appears to be a small unrelated entity (not a physical-security vendor).

**Conclusion:** no obvious incumbent conflict at preliminary depth. A formal trademark search ($300–$800 via a service like LegalZoom or direct USPTO) is advised before spending significant brand dollars — standard operating procedure for any serious name lock.
