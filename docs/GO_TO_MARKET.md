# Go-to-Market — Year 1

Three channels. One primary, one parallel, one opportunistic. Resist the urge to chase more until the first two compound.

---

## Channel 1 (primary, 60% of effort) — Direct founder-led enterprise

**Target:** 15–20 logos in Year 1 across logistics yards, colocation data centers, mid-size utilities.

**Motion:**
1. **Warm list of 50 target accounts.** Built from trade-press coverage of recent incidents, LinkedIn reconnaissance of Director of Security titles, conference attendee lists (ISC West, GSX, Data Center World, CS4CA).
2. **Founder direct outreach** — personalized email + LinkedIn + intro via security consultants. Target 10 conversations/week.
3. **Pilot offer:** 60-day free pilot at one site, Tessure-funded hardware and install. Customer agrees to: (a) case-study participation on success, (b) 12-month contract at list price on conversion.
4. **Design win → landing-and-expansion** — pilot at one site converts to 3–10 sites in months 4–9.
5. **Reference-driven second-logo capture** — every pilot customer named as a reference; next 5 deals cite them.

**Sales stack:** HubSpot (free tier Year 1), Close.com for calls, Gong for call recording (Year 2).

**Proof artifacts required:**
- 3 customer case studies (anonymized OK for the first two).
- 1 on-site demo video (scheduled Phase 9 asset).
- Technical architecture whitepaper (produce during Phase 1-2 run).
- Pilot-to-production readiness checklist (1 page).
- Privacy / data-handling white paper (critical for utilities).

## Channel 2 (parallel, 25% of effort) — Integrator channel

**Target:** 3–5 integrator partners in Year 1. 30% of pipeline by Year 2.

**Why:** The integrator channel controls **80%+** of physical-security deployments in enterprise. A founder-led direct motion does not scale past ~40 logos without them. Start seeding in Year 1 even if revenue impact is Year 2.

**Top integrator targets:**

| Integrator | Scope | Fit |
|---|---|---|
| **Convergint** | Global, $2B+ rev, strong data-center practice | Data-center + utilities. Highest leverage. |
| **Johnson Controls (Building Technologies)** | Global, $25B+ parent | Utilities + industrial. Slower to onboard. |
| **ADT Commercial (now Everon)** | US, $5B rev | Mid-market + retail + logistics. Easier onboarding. |
| **Carahsoft** | US gov/utility reseller | Federal + regulated utility procurement vehicle. |
| **Boutique: TAL Global, Pinkerton, Kroll** | Consultant-as-integrator | Estate + family-office segment. |

**Motion:**
1. **Founder-level partnership meeting** — pitch Tessure as differentiator for integrator's existing accounts.
2. **Deal registration program** — integrator logs deals, gets margin protection (25–35%) + lead-generation support.
3. **Joint field marketing** — co-branded one-pager, joint webinar, ride-alongs on installs.
4. **Certification path** — 2-day installer certification; Tessure-branded "Certified Fusion Integrator" badge.

**Do not chase** integrator exclusivity in Year 1. Take multiple partners; let the market pick which one scales.

## Channel 3 (opportunistic, 15% of effort) — Insurance-driven

**Target:** 2 insurance carriers with loss-control programs. 5+ referrals-into-pilots.

**Why:** Insurance pressure is a major customer trigger. Carriers like **Chubb (Masterpiece)**, **AIG Private Client**, **PURE Group**, **FM Global** (commercial), and **Zurich** (utility) write loss-control advisories that customers largely follow. If Tessure becomes a named option in their advisories, deal flow becomes inbound.

**Motion:**
1. **Target 2 carriers Year 1.** FM Global (commercial infra) and Chubb (estate).
2. **Pilot at a carrier-flagged risk** — carrier refers one of their policyholders for Tessure install at Tessure's cost; evaluate loss outcomes over 6 months.
3. **Carrier whitepaper co-authorship** — publish joint piece on "Verified Response as a Loss-Control Standard."
4. **Named-option status** — Tessure added to carrier's approved vendor list or advisory.

**Not a sales motion in Year 1.** This is Year-2 revenue. But the relationships start now.

## Anti-channels (explicit: we will not chase these Year 1)

- **DoD / federal defense.** Procurement is slow, scope-creeps toward DoD priorities, pulls us off civilian wedge. Revisit Year 3.
- **SMB retail / small business.** Ticket size too small; margins don't cover field service. Verkada's territory.
- **Consumer / residential.** Wrong product, wrong price. Deep Sentinel's graveyard.
- **International (outside US + Canada).** Deferred to Year 2+. Regulatory complexity + deployment logistics outsize the TAM vs US focus.

## Inbound / content marketing

Modest in Year 1. Purpose: give the direct motion air cover + SEO foundation for Year 2.

**Artifacts:**
- **Landing page** (v2 site from this playbook run)
- **Architecture whitepaper** (15 pages, downloadable)
- **Monthly blog**: 1 technical deep-dive + 1 market commentary per month
- **Podcast appearances**: Sean or CEO on 6 relevant podcasts Year 1 (Security Industry Magazine, SSI, IFSEC Global, Data Center Frontier)
- **Trade-show presence**: ISC West (booth + speaking), GSX (attend), Data Center World (attend, speak)
- **E-ISAC / ISF / ISA webinar slots**: 2–3 Year 1

**Not** investing in: paid search, paid social, display. Category is high-consideration; ROI won't appear until brand recognition exists.

### Analyst relations (Pass 3 addition)

Pass 3 research surfaced an analyst-coverage gap: no current (2025–2026) Gartner or Forrester **VMS Market Guide** is publicly indexed. The most recent Gartner VMS guide is 2017; Gartner's 2025 Cool Vendors in Cyber-Physical Systems Security is OT-cyber focused, not physical video/perimeter.

**Implication:** Tessure is selling into a category analysts haven't defined in ~8 years. No incumbent frame to fight against — opportunity to shape category language through early analyst briefings. But also no third-party validation to cite in enterprise sales.

**Year-1 actions:**
- **Month 4–6:** analyst briefings with Gartner, Forrester, Memoori, IHS Markit, and **IPVM** (the most relevant trade analyst for physical security).
- **Month 6:** publish architecture whitepaper in analyst-readable form.
- **Month 9–12:** push for inclusion in the next VMS market guide or CPS security market guide revision.
- Cost: one fractional analyst-relations consultant at ~$3k–5k/mo from Month 4.
